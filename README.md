# *Introduction to Molecular Modeling of DNA Aptamers*

This course presents a selection of concepts in Theoretical and Computational Chemistry that are required
to understand the modern molecular simulation methods   and their implementation in the form of computer tools.
Focus will be placed on their practical application for the construction of DNA aptamer models based on
molecular dynamics simulations. The diverse contents are introduced in theoretical & practical sessions 
in which the different stages of a computational protocol designed to explore the conformational space of DNA aptamers 
in aqueous solution are both explained and applied. Participants will use various software tools for the visualization, editing, execution 
and analysis of molecular dynamics simulations. 

Face-to-face teaching will take place in theoretical-practical sessions of 3 hours scheduled in a computer room suitable for the development of the course. 
As the proposed case studies progress, students will develop a mini-simulation project

The theoretical contents are presented in a summarized way, emphasizing the aspects 
whose full understanding is essential for the correct use of the simulation tools. The presentations will be supported by different 
teaching resources (teacher's notes in  interactive notebook format , graphic presentations, demonstrations of use, etc.). 

PhD students will address an aptamer sequence by applying a computational protocol that combines several programs and tasks. 
To this end, they will have the help of the APTAMD wrapper scripts 
to perform various tasks of preparation, execution and analysis of the simulations.
